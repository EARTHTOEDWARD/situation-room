# Multiplayer Vertical Slice — Milestone Notes

## Goal

Build the smallest playable multiplayer system that can produce:

- proxy–patron entrapment;
- divergence between public claims and private needs;
- internal hawk, statesman, and survival pressures;
- backchannel reciprocity;
- secret concessions and media exposure;
- bluff exhaustion;
- expiring leverage;
- escalation hysteresis.

## Implemented milestone

**Scenario:** The Burr  
**Roles:** Belarus, Russia, United States, European Union, United Nations  
**Rounds:** six  
**Interface:** one-human Solo command or single-device pass-and-play, with rules-based NPC seats

The solo Gulf 2026 game is retained as a training route from the landing page.

## Active design assumptions

1. **One shared ladder is enough for the vertical slice.** Political, military, proxy, and economic actions all feed a common ratchet rather than separate bilateral ladders.
2. **Simultaneous sealed plans matter.** A player cannot condition its current action on seeing another player’s current action.
3. **Structured private verbs are preferable to unconstrained text for the engine.** Players can talk freely, but matching mechanical intentions determine whether a deal forms.
4. **Internal politics should generate pressure, not require a second full game.** Three faction tracks and threshold events are the current complexity boundary.
5. **Authoritarian systems exchange public flexibility for elite fragility.** They are not simply given fewer penalties.
6. **Asset expiry is declared by the scenario.** The clock represents substitution, evidence accumulation, coalition decay, or a closing deployment window.

## What the prototype currently demonstrates

- Belarus can overclaim a negotiable border stake and privately seek a guarantee on its existential regime-survival stake.
- Russia can deepen entanglement, issue a blank cheque, restrain the proxy, or make support conditional.
- US reassurance can remain deliberately less specific than a public automatic-war commitment.
- EU sanctions have both coercive value and a coalition-unity clock.
- UN escrow and public inquiry create face capacity rather than military power.
- Private deals remain classified on the public log and are revealed only to participants or during the debrief unless they leak.
- Public threats have a two-round shelf life and lose credibility when unbacked.
- High rungs weld behind escalation and require face-saving capacity for descent.
- The replay seed and exported history make trajectories auditable.
- A single human can occupy any role while four deterministic NPCs complete legal sealed plans and carry the changing crisis state across all six rounds.

## Known limitations

- Privacy is social, not network-authenticated.
- Free-form promises are not parsed; players must encode binding terms using the available private actions.
- NPC policy is scenario-specific and heuristic, not strategic search or a language model.
- The shared ladder compresses several domains that may later need separate but coupled tracks.
- Only one role-specific scenario is implemented.
- The debrief scores interests operationally but has not yet been calibrated through human play data.
- No accessibility audit beyond keyboard modal controls, reduced-motion support, and responsive layout has been completed.

## Next decisive witness

Solo and observer runs remain synthetic diagnostics for mechanics, pacing, regression checks, and onboarding. They do not replace this human witness.

Run at least three uncoached five-player sessions and record:

- which public and private actions were selected;
- faction pressure and room events;
- unmatched versus matched backchannels;
- ladder and media trajectory;
- entanglement changes;
- asset-clock exhaustion;
- secret-deal leak rate;
- whether players could state the politically viable settlement before they could implement it.

### Positive result

If Belarus can entrap Russia in some runs but be restrained or bargained with in others, proceed to separate networked private clients and a second scenario.

### Negative result

If outcomes feel scripted or faction administration dominates play, simplify faction triggers and widen private action substitutability before adding China, Iran, or North Korea.
